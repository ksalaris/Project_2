Flooding Disasters Cost Billions

Flooding is the costliest and most common natural disaster in the U.S., claiming lives, inflicting financial losses on households and businesses, and straining the government agencies that provide flood response and relief. From 1980 to 2013, flooding cost Americans more than $260 billion in damage.

PEW Charitable Trusts

With the wealth of information available through the National Weather Service in regards to storm data, we were able to take a look specifically at data regarding flooding and flash flooding events over the last 20 years. Flooding as a whole is the costliest natural disaster within the United States, exasperated by urban development and climate change.


The number of floods annually has grown since 1999, as has the lead to billions in property and crop damage. It is also one of the deadliest natural hazards. The flooding is not limited to one corner of the country - because of the diversity of causes, it is encountered across the United States.


Our data shows two particularly significant events this year, located in Nebraska and Southern California. The Nebraska flooding was the result of excessive snow melt following an unseasonably warm run of days in March. California's flooding was the result of a series of floods. Generally speaking, the flooding incidents in the southern part of the US are a result of heavy rain, whereas northern parts of the country flood as a result of ice jams or snow melting.
